How the credit system works

Credits are how QBank keeps the question bank growing. You earn them by contributing, and you spend them to practise other people's questions.

Starting balance

  • Every new account starts with 10 credits.

Earning credits

  • +5 credits every time you add a new question.
  • +6 credits each time your question reaches another 10 completions (so 10, 20, 30 ... total completions).
  • +1 credit if you challenge an official answer and, in the author's judgement, both answers are correct.
  • +1 credit (pulled from the question's author) if you challenge and the original answer is judged wrong.

Spending credits

  • −2 credits to complete a question.
  • −1 credit instead, if you also rate the question's difficulty out of 10 while completing it. Ratings help other students.
  • −1 credit from the author's balance if a challenge proves the original answer was wrong. The challenger gets that credit.

Why the design?

The point of credits is to nudge the community toward adding questions. Practising other people's work costs a little, but contributing your own (or helping rate or correct other work) pays back more than it costs. The 10-completion bonus means the most useful questions keep rewarding their authors over time.

Answer challenges

After completing a question you'll see the official answer. If you believe it's wrong, submit a challenge with your proposed answer. The question's author reviews it and picks one of three outcomes:

  1. Original wrong — the official answer is replaced with yours, and 1 credit moves from the author to you.
  2. Both correct — you get +1 credit as a reward for contributing an alternative.
  3. Rejected — no credit changes hands.
